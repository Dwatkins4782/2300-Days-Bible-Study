## PART 1: THE VISION OF DANIEL 8

### The Setting
Daniel 8 records a vision given to the prophet Daniel in the third year of King Belshazzar's reign (approximately 550 B.C.). Daniel is transported in vision to the citadel of Susa in the province of Elam.

**Read: Daniel 8:1-4**

### The Ram (Medo-Persia)
Daniel sees a ram with two horns — one higher than the other, with the higher one coming up last. The ram pushes westward, northward, and southward, and no beast could stand before it.

**Interpretation (given by the angel Gabriel):**
> *"The ram which thou sawest having two horns are the kings of Media and Persia."* — Daniel 8:20

- **Two horns** = Media and Persia
- **Higher horn coming up last** = Persia rising to dominance after Media
- **Pushing in three directions** = Medo-Persia's conquests

### The He-Goat (Greece)
**Read: Daniel 8:5-8**

A male goat comes from the west with a notable horn between its eyes. It strikes the ram, breaks its two horns, and tramples it. Then the great horn is broken, and four notable horns come up in its place.

**Interpretation:**
> *"And the rough goat is the king of Grecia: and the great horn that is between his eyes is the first king."* — Daniel 8:21-22

- **The goat** = Greece
- **The great horn** = Alexander the Great
- **The four horns** = Four divisions of Alexander's empire (Cassander, Lysimachus, Seleucus, Ptolemy)

### The Little Horn (Rome — Pagan and Papal)
**Read: Daniel 8:9-12**

Out of one of the four horns comes a "little horn" that grows exceedingly great toward the south, east, and the "pleasant land" (Palestine).

**Key characteristics of this little horn power:**

| # | Characteristic | Verse |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Grows "exceeding great" — greater than Medo-Persia and Greece | v. 9 |
| 2 | Extends toward the south, east, and the pleasant land | v. 9 |
| 3 | Opposes the "host of heaven" — God's people | v. 10 |
| 4 | Magnifies itself against the "Prince of the host" — Christ | v. 11 |
| 5 | Removes the "daily" (tamid) — Christ's continual priestly ministry | v. 11 |
| 6 | Casts down the "place of His sanctuary" | v. 11 |
| 7 | Casts truth to the ground | v. 12 |
| 8 | Practices and prospers | v. 12 |

**SDA Interpretation — Rome in two phases:**

- **Pagan Rome** — Destroyed Jerusalem and the earthly temple (A.D. 70), persecuted God's people, and crucified the "Prince of the host" — Jesus Christ.
- **Papal Rome** — Substituted a counterfeit system of mediation (the Mass, the confessional, priestly absolution, saints as intercessors) for Christ's continual (*tamid*) high-priestly ministry in the heavenly sanctuary. It "cast truth to the ground" by introducing unbiblical doctrines.

## PART 2: THE QUESTION AND THE ANSWER

### The Holy Ones Speak
**Read: Daniel 8:13-14**

> *"How long shall be the vision concerning the daily sacrifice, and the transgression of desolation, to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot? And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed."*

- **The question:** How long will the little horn's oppressive activities continue?
- **The answer:** 2,300 days, then the sanctuary will be cleansed
  - Hebrew: *tsadaq* — "justified," "vindicated," "restored to its rightful state," "made right"

### The Year-Day Principle

In apocalyptic prophecy, a **prophetic day = a literal year**.

**Biblical basis:**
- **Numbers 14:34** — "each day for a year"
- **Ezekiel 4:6** — "I have appointed thee each day for a year"

**Why the year-day principle applies here:**
1. The vision spans multiple world empires (centuries, not 6.3 years)
2. Gabriel says the vision concerns "the time of the end" (Daniel 8:17) and "many days" (Daniel 8:26)
3. The Hebrew *ereb boqer* ("evening-morning") recalls creation language and daily sanctuary sacrifices

**Therefore: 2,300 prophetic days = 2,300 literal years**

## PART 3: THE CONNECTION TO DANIEL 9 — THE KEY TO THE STARTING DATE

### Daniel's Confusion and Gabriel's Return
**Read: Daniel 8:26-27**

At the end of Daniel 8, Daniel is sick and astonished. Gabriel had explained the ram, goat, and little horn — but had **not** explained the 2,300 days.

**Read: Daniel 9:20-23**

Gabriel returns:
> *"O Daniel, I am now come forth to give thee skill and understanding... therefore understand the matter, and consider the vision."* — Daniel 9:22-23

**Which vision?** The one left unexplained — the 2,300 days from Daniel 8.

### The 70 Weeks: "Cut Off" from the 2,300 Days
**Read: Daniel 9:24**

> *"Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people and upon thy holy city..."*

- Hebrew *chatak* = "**cut off**" or "decreed"
- Cut off from what? The only larger time period under discussion: **the 2,300 days**
- The 70 weeks (490 years) form the **first portion** of the 2,300 years

**The 70 weeks and the 2,300 days share the same starting point.**

### The Starting Point: 457 B.C.
**Read: Daniel 9:25**

> *"From the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem unto the Messiah the Prince..."*

The decree to "restore and build Jerusalem" was issued by **Artaxerxes I in 457 B.C.** (Ezra 7:12-26).

**Why this decree and not earlier ones?**

| Decree | Date | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Cyrus | 536 B.C. | Temple only |
| Darius | 519 B.C. | Temple completion |
| **Artaxerxes** | **457 B.C.** | **Full restoration of city, civil & judicial functions** |

Only Artaxerxes' decree authorized the **full restoration** of Jerusalem's civil and judicial governance.

### Segment 1: Seven Weeks (49 years) — 457 B.C. to 408 B.C.

## PART 5: THE TERMINUS — 1844 AND THE CLEANSING OF THE SANCTUARY

### The Calculation


> *"Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed."*

### What Sanctuary?
The earthly temple was destroyed in A.D. 70 — long before 1844. The sanctuary cleansed in 1844 must be **the heavenly sanctuary**.

**Key texts:**
| Reference | Teaching |
|---|---|
| Hebrews 8:1-2 | Christ ministers in the "true tabernacle" in heaven |
| Hebrews 9:23-24 | The earthly was a "pattern" of the heavenly |

| Revelation 11:19 | The temple of God opened in heaven, with the ark of His testament |

### What Does "Cleansing" Mean?
Understanding requires studying the **Day of Atonement** (Yom Kippur) — Leviticus 16.

**The Earthly Day of Atonement:**
- Occurred once a year — 10th day of the 7th month (Tishri)
- A day of **judgment** — anyone not afflicting his soul was "cut off" (Leviticus 23:29)
- The high priest entered the Most Holy Place for the only time all year
- Blood sprinkled on the mercy seat — cover of the Ark containing the Ten Commandments
- The sanctuary was cleansed from accumulated sins transferred throughout the year
- Sins placed on the scapegoat (*azazel*) — led into the wilderness (symbol of Satan)

**The Antitype — The Heavenly Day of Atonement (Beginning 1844):**
1. Christ entering the Most Holy Place to begin the **investigative judgment**
2. Examination of the records ("books" — Daniel 7:10; Revelation 20:12)
3. Vindication of God's character before the watching universe (Romans 3:4, 26)
4. Blotting out of sins for those found in Christ (Acts 3:19-20)

## PART 9: COMMON OBJECTIONS AND RESPONSES

### Objection 1: "The 2,300 days are literal days, not years."
**Response:** Literal days (~6.3 years) cannot account for a vision spanning Medo-Persia, Greece, and Rome — centuries of history. The year-day principle is biblically established (Numbers 14:34; Ezekiel 4:6). The term *ereb boqer* ("evenings-mornings") ties to the sanctuary cycle and creation language.

### Objection 2: "The sanctuary was Antiochus Epiphanes' desecration."
**Response:** Antiochus IV does not fulfill the prophecy because:
1. He was a minor king, not "exceeding great" — far less powerful than Medo-Persia or Greece
2. His desecration lasted ~3 years, not 2,300 years
3. Gabriel says the vision is for "the time of the end" (Daniel 8:17, 19)
4. The little horn grows after the four horns — Rome succeeded divided Greece, not a minor Seleucid king
5. The "Prince of the host" fits Christ, not a Jewish priest

### Objection 3: "Daniel 9 is not connected to Daniel 8."
**Response:** The Hebrew *chatak* ("cut off") implies cutting from a larger whole. Gabriel references a prior vision (Daniel 9:21-23; cf. 8:16, 26-27). The unexplained element of Daniel 8 was the time prophecy. Thematic continuity — sanctuary, Messiah, time, judgment — binds the chapters inseparably.

### Objection 4: "There is no heavenly sanctuary."
**Response:** Hebrews is built on the reality of the heavenly sanctuary (8:1-5; 9:11, 23-24). Revelation depicts the heavenly temple with altar of incense (8:3-4), lampstands (1:12), and Ark of the Covenant (11:19). The earthly was always a "copy and shadow" (Hebrews 8:5).
