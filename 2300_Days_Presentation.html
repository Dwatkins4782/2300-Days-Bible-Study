The Sanctuary — God's Blueprint

"Let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them." — Exodus 25:8

The Structure

CourtyardAltar of burnt offering, Laver
Holy PlaceLampstand, Table of showbread, Incense altar
Most Holy PlaceArk of Covenant (Ten Commandments), Mercy seat

Moses built it "according to the pattern" shown on the mount (Exodus 25:9, 40; Hebrews 8:5)

Two Services

DAILY (Tamid): Morning & evening lamb (Exodus 29:38-42). Individual sin offerings — sin transferred to sanctuary (Lev. 4). Incense morning/evening (Exodus 30:7-8).

YEARLY (Day of Atonement): High priest enters Most Holy Place. Sanctuary cleansed from accumulated sins (Lev. 16). A day of judgment — "afflict your souls" (Lev. 23:29).

Daniel 8:13 asks a sanctuary question. Daniel 8:14 gives a sanctuary answer.
This is, from first verse to last, a sanctuary prophecy.

Part 1: The Vision of Daniel 8

A vision given to Daniel ~550 B.C. at the citadel of Susa

The Ram — Medo-Persia

Two horns (Media & Persia), the higher coming up last. Pushes westward, northward, southward.

"The ram which thou sawest having two horns are the kings of Media and Persia." Daniel 8:20

The He-Goat — Greece

Notable horn between its eyes. Strikes and tramples the ram. The great horn breaks; four arise.

"The rough goat is the king of Grecia: and the great horn... is the first king." Daniel 8:21

The Little Horn — Rome (Pagan & Papal)

Grows "exceeding great." Opposes the "Prince of the host" (Christ). Removes the daily (tamid) — an assault on Christ's continual heavenly priesthood. Casts truth to the ground.

Pagan Rome: Crucified Christ, destroyed the temple (A.D. 70) • Papal Rome: Substituted earthly priests for the one heavenly Priest (EGW: Christ in His Sanctuary, p. 61)

Part 2: The Question & the Answer

"How long shall be the vision concerning the daily sacrifice, and the transgression of desolation, to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot? And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed." Daniel 8:13-14

The Question (v. 13)

Every word is sanctuary language: "daily sacrifice" (tamid), "sanctuary," "trodden under foot." This is a sanctuary question.

The Answer (v. 14)

2,300 days — then the sanctuary shall be cleansed.

Hebrew tsadaq: "justified," "vindicated," "restored to its rightful state." LXX: katharisthesetai (same root as Heb. 9:23).

The Year-Day Principle: 1 prophetic day = 1 literal year (Numbers 14:34; Ezekiel 4:6)

2,300 prophetic days = 2,300 literal years

Part 3: The Connection to Daniel 9

The key to the starting date

Gabriel Returns

Daniel 8 ends with the 2,300 days left unexplained. In Daniel 9, Gabriel returns to explain "the vision."

"O Daniel, I am now come forth to give thee skill and understanding... consider the vision." Daniel 9:22-23

70 Weeks "Cut Off"

Hebrew chatak = "cut off" from the 2,300 years. The 70 weeks (490 years) form the first portion.

"Anoint the most Holy" (mashach qodesh qodashim) = inaugurate the heavenly sanctuary (cf. Exodus 30:26-29; Heb. 9:11-12, 24).

The Starting Point: 457 B.C.

The decree of Artaxerxes I (Ezra 7:12-26) to restore and build Jerusalem. Confirmed by Ptolemy's Canon, the Elephantine Papyri, and Greek Olympiad records.

Part 9: Common Objections

"The 2,300 days are literal, not years."

Literal days (~6.3 years) cannot span Medo-Persia, Greece, and Rome. The year-day principle is biblically established (Num. 14:34; Eze. 4:6). Gabriel says the vision is for "the time of the end."

"This is about Antiochus Epiphanes."

Antiochus was a minor king (not "exceeding great"), his desecration lasted ~3 years (not 2,300), and the "Prince of the host" fits Christ, not a Jewish priest.

"Daniel 9 is not connected to Daniel 8."

Hebrew chatak means "cut off" from a larger whole. Gabriel references the prior unexplained vision. Thematic continuity binds them inseparably.

"There is no heavenly sanctuary."

Hebrews 8-9 is built on the heavenly sanctuary's reality. Revelation depicts its altar (8:3-4), lampstands (1:12), and Ark (11:19).
