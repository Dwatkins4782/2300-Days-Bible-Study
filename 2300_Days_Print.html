Part 1: The Vision of Daniel 8

A vision given to Daniel ~550 B.C. at the citadel of Susa

The Ram — Medo-Persia

Two horns (Media & Persia), the higher coming up last. Pushes westward, northward, southward.

"The ram which thou sawest having two horns are the kings of Media and Persia." Daniel 8:20

The He-Goat — Greece

Notable horn between its eyes. Strikes the ram. Great horn breaks; four arise in its place.

"The rough goat is the king of Grecia: and the great horn... is the first king." Daniel 8:21

The Little Horn — Rome (Pagan & Papal)

Grows "exceeding great." Opposes the "Prince of the host" (Christ). Removes the daily (tamid). Casts truth to the ground.

Pagan Rome: Crucified Christ, destroyed the temple (A.D. 70)  •  Papal Rome: Substituted a counterfeit mediation system for Christ's priestly ministry

Part 2: The Question & the Answer

"How long shall be the vision concerning the daily sacrifice, and the transgression of desolation, to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot? And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed." Daniel 8:13-14

The Question

How long will the little horn's oppressive activities — removing the daily, trampling the sanctuary and God's people — continue?

The Answer

2,300 days — then the sanctuary shall be cleansed.

Hebrew tsadaq: "justified," "vindicated," "restored to its rightful state"

The Year-Day Principle: In apocalyptic prophecy, 1 prophetic day = 1 literal year
(Numbers 14:34 • Ezekiel 4:6)

2,300 prophetic days = 2,300 literal years

Part 3: The Connection to Daniel 9

The key to the starting date

Gabriel Returns

Daniel 8 ends with the 2,300 days left unexplained. Daniel is sick and "none understood it." (Dan 8:27)

In Daniel 9, Gabriel returns:

"O Daniel, I am now come forth to give thee skill and understanding... consider the vision." Daniel 9:22-23

70 Weeks "Cut Off"

Hebrew chatak = "cut off"

The 70 weeks (490 years) are cut off from the 2,300 years — forming the first portion of the longer prophecy.

Both prophecies share the same starting point

The Starting Point: 457 B.C.

The decree of Artaxerxes I (Ezra 7:12-26) to restore and build Jerusalem — not merely the temple, but the city and its civil governance. This is the only decree that authorized full restoration of civil and judicial functions.

The earthly temple was destroyed in A.D. 70 — long before 1844.

Therefore, the sanctuary cleansed in 1844 must be the heavenly sanctuary — "the true tabernacle which the Lord pitched, and not man" (Heb. 8:2).

Biblical Evidence

  • Hebrews 8:1-2 — Christ ministers in the "true tabernacle"
  • Hebrews 9:23-24 — Earthly was a "pattern" of the heavenly
  • Revelation 11:19 — The ark of His testament seen in heaven

If the first 490 years were fulfilled with pinpoint accuracy,
we can trust that the terminus in 1844 is equally reliable.

Part 9: Common Objections & Responses

"The 2,300 days are literal, not years."

Literal days (~6.3 years) cannot span Medo-Persia, Greece, and Rome. The year-day principle is biblically established (Num. 14:34; Eze. 4:6). Gabriel says the vision is for "the time of the end."

"This is about Antiochus Epiphanes."

Antiochus was a minor king (not "exceeding great"), his desecration lasted ~3 years (not 2,300), and the "Prince of the host" fits Christ, not a Jewish priest.

"Daniel 9 is not connected to Daniel 8."

Hebrew chatak means "cut off" from a larger whole. Gabriel references the prior unexplained vision. Thematic continuity binds the chapters inseparably.

"There is no heavenly sanctuary."

Hebrews 8-9 is built on the heavenly sanctuary's reality. Revelation depicts its altar (8:3-4), lampstands (1:12), and Ark (11:19). The earthly was always a "copy and shadow."
