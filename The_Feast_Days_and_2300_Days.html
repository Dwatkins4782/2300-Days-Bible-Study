From “Holy Day” to “Holiday”

The Word Tells the Story

The English word “holiday” originates from the Old English hāligdæg — a combination of hālig (holy) and dæg (day). It literally meant “holy day.”

PeriodTermMeaning
~950 A.D. — Old EnglishHāligdægStrictly religious festivals, sacred days of rest
12th–15th Century — Middle EnglishHalidayRetains religious meaning but begins to include leisure and celebration
16th Century — Modern English“Holiday”Expands to cover any celebratory day, secular or religious
Today“Holiday”Synonymous with vacation, time off, recreation — the “holy” is gone
Key Takeaway

The very word reveals what happened. God’s sacred, set-apart days were gradually secularized, paganized, and emptied of their prophetic meaning. What was once “holy” became merely “fun.” But God still has His appointed times — and they still carry prophetic weight.

The Mo’edim — God’s Appointed Times

Hebrew: מוֹעֵד — Mo’ed (Strong’s H4150)

This word means far more than “feast” or “festival.” Its root meaning is “an appointment — a fixed time or season.”

Full definition: Properly, an appointment; a fixed time or season; specifically, a festival; by implication, an assembly convened for a definite purpose; the place of meeting; also a signal appointed beforehand. Translated as: appointed sign, appointed time, solemn assembly, congregation, set feast, due season, set time.

God didn’t give Israel “holidays.” He gave them divine appointments — set times with prophetic purpose, each one pointing to a specific act in the plan of salvation.

Statutes and Ordinances

Statutes (H2708 — chûqqâh): Feminine of H2706: appointed custom, manner, ordinance, statute. Used 104 times. These are God’s permanent prescriptions.

Ordinances (H2706 — chôq): An enactment; an appointment of time, space, quantity, labor, or usage. Used 127 times. These are God’s decreed boundaries.

“These are the statutes and judgments, which ye shall observe to do in the land, which the LORD God of thy fathers giveth thee to possess it, all the days that ye live upon the earth.”

— Deuteronomy 12:1 KJV

“And the LORD appointed a set time [H4150], saying, To morrow the LORD shall do this thing in the land.”

— Exodus 9:5 KJV

The True New Year — Abib/Nisan

The world celebrates January 1 as New Year’s Day. But where did this come from?

The Pagan Origin: January 1 was established in honor of Janus, the two-faced Roman god of beginnings, gates, and transitions. The Julian calendar (46 B.C.) and later Pope Gregory XIII’s calendar (1582) both enshrined this pagan date. We inherited a calendar from Rome, not from God.

God’s New Year: The Month of Abib

“This month shall be unto you the beginning of months: it shall be the first month of the year to you.”

— Exodus 12:2 KJV

“Remember this day, in which ye came out from Egypt, out of the house of bondage... This day came ye out in the month Abib.”

— Exodus 13:3–4 KJV

“Observe the month of Abib, and keep the passover unto the LORD thy God: for in the month of Abib the LORD thy God brought thee forth out of Egypt by night.”

— Deuteronomy 16:1 KJV

Abib (later called Nisan) falls in late March to early April — the time of spring, new life, and the Passover. God’s year begins with redemption, not with a pagan deity.

“The time of the Passover corresponded to the close of March or the beginning of April, and the whole land was bright with flowers, and glad with the song of birds.”

— The Desire of Ages, p. 75

Seasons, Seeds, and the Harvest

God’s Calendar Is Written in Nature

Even creation testifies to God’s appointed times. The seasons themselves are a parable:

Fall & Winter

Everything dies. Leaves fall. Animals hibernate — entering a state resembling death. The earth rests. Cold and darkness prevail.

Spring

New life springs forth. Animals awaken. Seeds germinate. Flowers appear. The very word “spring” means to leap into life.

“For, lo, the winter is past, the rain is over and gone; the flowers appear on the earth; the time of the singing of birds is come... Arise, my love, my fair one, and come away.”

— Song of Solomon 2:11–13 KJV

“Behold, the former things are come to pass, and new things do I declare: before they spring forth I tell you of them.”

— Isaiah 42:9 KJV

The Parable of the Sower and the Harvest

Jesus built His teaching on the agricultural cycle — the very cycle that God’s feasts are built upon:

“For the earth bringeth forth fruit of herself; first the blade, then the ear, after that the full corn in the ear. But when the fruit is brought forth, immediately he putteth in the sickle, because the harvest is come.”

— Mark 4:28–29 KJV

“Put ye in the sickle, for the harvest is ripe: come, get you down; for the press is full, the fats overflow; for their wickedness is great.”

— Joel 3:13 KJV

“When the character of Christ shall be perfectly reproduced in His people, then He will come to claim them as His own.”

— Christ’s Object Lessons, p. 69

The feasts trace the growth cycle of the gospel seed — from planting (Passover) to harvest (Tabernacles).

Take thou away from me the noise of thy songs; for I will not hear the melody of thy viols.”

— Amos 5:21–23 KJV

“Bring no more vain oblations; incense is an abomination unto me; the new moons and sabbaths, the calling of assemblies, I cannot away with; it is iniquity, even the solemn meeting. Your new moons and your appointed feasts my soul hateth: they are a trouble unto me; I am weary to bear them.”

The Rebellion Pattern — From Golden Calf to Today

“And he received them at their hand, and fashioned it with a graving tool, after he had made it a molten calf: and they said, These be thy gods, O Israel... And Aaron made proclamation, and said, To morrow is a feast to the LORD.”

— Exodus 32:4–5 KJV

Notice the pattern: Aaron didn’t say “tomorrow is a feast to Baal.” He said “a feast to the LORD” — but it was pagan worship dressed in the Lord’s name. This is the essence of syncretism: calling pagan practices “Christian.”

The pattern repeats: Baal-peor (Numbers 25:2–5) — Israel joined pagan feasting and bowed to pagan gods, provoking God’s fierce anger. The same spirit is at work when pagan-rooted celebrations (Christmas, Easter, Halloween, January 1) are baptized with Christian names while God’s actual appointed times are ignored or despised.

“Yea, the stork in the heaven knoweth her appointed times; and the turtle and the crane and the swallow observe the time of their coming; but my people know not the judgment of the LORD.”

— Jeremiah 8:7 KJV

Even the animals know God’s appointed times — but His people have forgotten them.

From Appointed Times to the 2,300-Day Prophecy

We have established that God operates on a precise prophetic calendar — His mo’edim, His appointed times. These are not relics of the Old Testament. They are the prophetic scaffolding of the plan of salvation.

Now we must ask: Is there a prophecy in Scripture that ties all the feast days together into one grand prophetic timeline?

The answer is YES.

It is the longest time prophecy in the Bible — and it encompasses the fulfillment of every feast, from Passover to the Day of Atonement to the final harvest.

“Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed.”

— Daniel 8:14 KJV

The spring feasts were fulfilled within this prophecy. The Day of Atonement was fulfilled at its terminus. The Feast of Tabernacles awaits its fulfillment beyond it. Let us now open this prophecy.

A Baptist farmer from upstate New York who, beginning around 1816, conducted a systematic verse-by-verse study of Scripture. His findings:

  • Correctly applied the year-day principle
  • Correctly connected Daniel 8 and Daniel 9
  • Correctly identified 457 B.C. as the starting date
  • Correctly calculated the 2,300 years ending ~1843–1844
